1. Master the New "Tap-to-Pay" Technology
The most recent major development in Tanzania is the rollout of tap-to-pay using smartphones. Vodacom and Safaricom recently launched this feature for Android users, allowing them to tap their phones at any Visa-enabled terminal . Integrating this now positions your app as a leader in contactless payments.

2. Prioritize Seamless Cross-Border Payments
Cross-border trade within East Africa and with global markets like China and the UAE is a massive opportunity. The new M-PESA Global Payment system allows users to pay merchants in China via Alipay and in Dubai via TerraPay . Adding a feature that simplifies these international transactions will attract businesses and frequent travelers.

3. Build for the Unbanked with Smart Onboarding and Agency Banking
Financial inclusion is key in Tanzania. The most successful local apps feature smart onboarding that uses a phone number and NIDA biometrics to open an account instantly, removing traditional barriers . Additionally, incorporating agency banking features (cash-in/cash-out for agents) can help you reach users in rural areas who may not have a traditional bank account .

4. Go Beyond Payments to "Banking as a Lifestyle"
To retain users, your app needs to help them manage their financial health. Successful local apps have introduced features like "Kibubu," an automated savings tool that lets users set aside a percentage of every transaction . By rewarding users for saving (e.g., "Qwikrewards") and providing clear spending insights, you turn your app into an indispensable daily tool.
